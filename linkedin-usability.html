LinkedIn Usability Study

Identify issues in LinkedIn's onboarding process

OVERVIEW

Data has indicated that LinkedIn was experiencing new user retention problem. As a UX Researcher, I along with 3 other teammates conducted usability test and made corresponding design recommendations. Based on our findings, we recommended LinkedIn to redesign the onboarding experience according to our recommendation.

PROCESS

Problem

As suggested by external data, LinkedIn was experiencing problems with retaining their new users. An investigation into their onboarding process might reveal reasons and direction to enhance new users experience and retain them.

Research Question

Starting the project, we as a team looked at the website together and defined onboarding LinkedIn as creating profile and starting to search for jobs. Then, to guide our project, we asked:

  • Can the users create an account and profile?
  • Can the users search for the jobs they want?

For each research question, we specific wanted to know what factors were preventing them from having a good experience overall.

Heuristic Evaluation

Keeping our research questions in mind, we interacted with the account/profile creating process & initial job searching in detail and tried to spot any potential issue from our standpoint. By doing so, we will be able to have more information to help set up the usability test with real users.

We used Norman Nielson’s heuristics to guide our process.

Overall, our finding suggested that LinkedIn’s onboarding process had the following problems:

  1. It failed to inform users of the current state of the process (e.g. where they are and where they are going). This may cause confusion and frustration as users don’t know where they are in the signup process.
  2. It limited users’ freedom, e.g. limited options in allowing users to identify their industries. This may cause frustration as users may not be able to accurately identify what they do.

Thus, when designing usability test, we paid special attention to users’ expectation and the interaction flow the users experienced.

Study Design

Drawing from the insights from the heuristic evaluation, we created a usability with 4 tasks, centering around the onboarding experience. We created a dummy resume and persona for the purpose of usability testing, so the error caused by the differences in personal information can be eliminated. We conducted the studies in a formal usability lab, using Morae to record all the session for later data analysis.

Task 1: Create a LinkedIn account

Task 2: Create a profile using the dummy resume

Task 3: Connect with predefined individuals

Task 4: Find a predefined job posting

All the tasks were framed in scenarios that were narrated by the moderator.

‍

Participants

Since we were testing LinkedIn’s onboarding experience, we focused on people who had little to no experience with LinkedIn, but had the need to look for a job. We reasoned that college students were an appropriate target group because many of them were seeking for jobs and not all of them used LinkedIn. They were also accessible to us. A screening survey was used for recruitment

Overall, 5 participants from University of Washington, Seattle were recruited. The participants were in the age range of 18 - 24 years old and of different gender (3 females and 2 males).

Findings & Design 
Recommendation

1. Sign up process is too lengthy & intrusive

More than half of the participants encountered issue with the sign-up process and asked the moderator for help. They reported that the signup process was too lengthy and intrusive, because it asked for too many personal information such as phone number, personal contacts, etc.

Design Recommendation:
  • Shorten the signup process by not asking unnecessary information - By not asking unnecessary information such as phone number, the signup process is shorten and less intrusive to the users.
  • Show progress bar - progress bar informs users where they are in the signup process, so they will feel less anxious and uncertain about the process 

‍

2. Lots of hidden or grayed out UI elements

Grayed out elements creates unnecessary confusion to users and may stop them from even finishing the onboarding process.

‍

Design Recommendation:

we recommend avoid graying out interactive elements and let users to decide what to do. Interactive elements should also be put in expected locations.

3. Inconsistency on UI and search results

Novice users (users with few connections) were shown with different UI and search results compared to experienced users (users with many connections). This created confusion to new users as it made it difficult for them to connect with people they did not directly know.
