Glimpse

Visualizing fear, motivation, and goal

ABOUT GLIMPSE

Glimpse is a light sculpture visualizing “fear drive” – something personally important that causes people fear yet motivates them to achieve certain goals. On the surface level, “fear drive” may seem terrifying and so cause people to avoid confronting them, Through Glimpse, users could learn to see their “fear drive” as an ordinary yet aesthetic object. With the use of affective computing techniques, Glimpse encourages its users to accept and actively contemplate about their fear drive.

Glimpse consists of two parts – 1) a paper triangular prism with cut-out abstract text that describes users’ fear drive, 2) RGB LED that reflects the emotion behind the text. Each light sculpture represents distinct individual’s fear drive.

INSTALLATION

I installed Glimpse at a public room in my department building. People were welcomed to come in and view Glimpse as they pleased. I was present for the entire installation to gather feedback and to answer viewers' questions. Viewers showed great interest in Glimpse and would like to have one in their own room.

FEAR DRIVE

Fear drive contains three dimensions – fear, motivation, and goal. They intertwine with each other in a way that is difficult to distinct one from another.

This is inductively generated from in-depth interview with 5 people as well as literature review.

METHOD SPEC

How was each Glimpse light sculpture made?

Learn More
Interview
I interviewed 2 people, with a focus on what was personally important to them, the potential fear associated to this important thing, what was the goal they were striving for, etc.
Coding the interview
After the interview, I systematically coded the interview data into categories of “fear”, “motivation” and “goal” for each interviewee.
Word Cloud
Then, I generated word cloud for each category of interview data. This was to give the users of Glimpse enough room of interpretation to understand their fear drive.
Text cut-out
The word cloud was then cut out from paper through laser cutting, and the paper folded into a triangular prism, each side representing one dimension of fear drive. The user did not know which side is which, allowing for more interpretation.
Sentiment value to RGB value
The interview data generated above was then put into IBM Watson Tone Analyzer, which output the sentiment value of the input text, e.g. sadness 0.67, anger 0.2. The sentiment value was then translated into RGB value using a formula which represented the proportion of each sentiment, e.g. a 0.7 sadness value will generate 255 * 0.7 blue value in RGB LED. If there is more than one sentiment, the final RGB value represents the mixed results of the sentiments.
Anchoring light to corresponding side of the prism
Each RGB LED was then installed and anchored towards the side of the prism it corresponded, representing the complex emotion one’s fear drive consists of.

Reflecting Forward

Conceptualization // Embrace Ambiguity // Design // Installation // Pondering

This project nicely combined my philosophy background as well as my design background. From this project, I have learned to think by doing, even if that question was as complex as human nature. By designing Glimpse, I really feel that I have understood my "fear drive" a lot more. This project led me to ask - what would happen if people put Glimpse in their own room? What thoughts would be evoked if one can visually see their own fear drive constantly? I plan to conduct a long-term study examining the effect of Glimpse on people soon. Stay tuned!
