ColorAway

An exploratory design to promote solo travelers' mindfulness

OVERVIEW

In this project, we researched and designed ColorAway to enhance solo travelers' mindfulness through interacting with modified photos. In a team of three, I led, conducted, and managed the research, design, and prototyping processes. I along with my team generated a paper and submitted it to CHI Conference 2018. This project is advised by Mania Orand.

A previous related work I led was published in CHI Conference 2017, and received 4th place at its Student Research Competition. CHI Conference is considered as one of the highest-rank conference in Human-Computer Interaction.

Understanding Solo Travelers

Build on a larger study regarding the needs and technology use of solo travelers

We thoroughly analyzed and identified solo travelers' needs, and decided to focus on
the mindfulness aspect of solo travelers.

Mindfulness

Definition: Recollect past learning and evaluate thoughts so to become a better self.

After a thorough review of Western Psychology and Buddhist Literature, we defined mindfulness as the ability to focus on one's experiences so to evaluate them. There are two important elements within the practice of mindfulness - "recollection" (remember, recall, and recollect past internal learning) and "evaluation" (reflexively contemplate and understand one’s thoughts and feelings to distinguish beneficial from harmful qualities, so one to decide what to pursue and what not to pursue).

Design Question
How can we design a system that helps travelers learn more about their experiences and themselves?

Mindfulness Design Strategy 1

Recollection - Ask travelers to revisit their photos and pick three most meaningful pictures
“ I kind of thought about what is special about each of these places. Like, how did, what was, if I have three photo represent my personal, internal transitions, what would they be? I remember having a personal moment of time. And then I found a photo representative. - solo traveler 1

Mindfulness Design Strategy 2

Evaluation - User various ambiguity techniques to make the photos abstract to trigger imagination and meaning making

“ The first [photo], I was thinking how amazing it was to stand in front of other people, because that time I, it's only been two years since I've been in the U.S., two years and a half or so, so I was not very used to standing in front of other people and speaking and introducing myself in English. So this was actually personally important for me, because it gave me lots of confidence for me to stand in front of others who are not sharing the same language with me, so, umm, kind of unconsciously I just put lots of stars. - solo traveler 2
